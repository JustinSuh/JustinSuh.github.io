My List of Things to Know

I'm going to create a list of things that you may have already heard. I've gone through a ton of websites and I hope to be able to impart enough knowledge onto you. I'll be focusing mainly on the subject of staying safe.

  • Be Excited

    As much as I would love to embark on this trip with you, I can't. From the numberous blogs, websites, and posts I've read, travelling alone is a really great opportunity. I'm sure when all is said and done, you'll have had a great time.

  • Follow Your Gut/Intuition

    Follow your gut instinct, if it feels like a bad situation, don't wait around. Move to a better situation. Trust yourself, and don't fall into panic in unfamiliar situations. If you ever feel yourself panicking, just take a couple of deep breaths and calm yourself down.

  • Deter Creeps

    This advice I found to be rather common, and it actually makes a lot of sense. Wear a wedding ring to keep some of the creepers at bay. It'll definitely help.

  • Don't bring valuables

    This one is pretty obvious, but leave any flashy jewelry at home, along with that huge purse if you have one. Try to keep electronics and your wallet out of sight.

  • Try to Move as a Group

    It's often more comforting to find co-ed groups to travel with for short stints. By going to a hostel and chatting it up with people, you're sure to find some adventures to join up with. You can also let these people know where you may be at times to make sure that they can recognize if you don't return in a timely manner.

  • Your Weapons

    Leave the mace/weapons/etc at home. It won't cross borders. If you can buy some across borders, feel free to do so. Whatever helps you feel safe. I strongly recommend keeping a whistle handy. This will most likely deter a majority of people who mean harm.

  • Be Confident

    Walk with your chin up and stay alert, even when you just arrive. If you get hassled, pretend you have somewhere to go. You can walk up to another foreigner and act like friends as well. Most will understand.

  • Martial Arts

    That being said, if you have some spare time, some basic women-specific hand-to-hand defense training can help. If anything, it might give you some more confidence out there and help keep that chin up. This isn't totally necessary. It's more of a "nice to have".

  • Plan Your Routes

    Choose your routes and plans smartly. If you're going out, go out early and get back early, or invite some hostel mates. Don't go out late and take short cuts back to the hostel. It's bad for business, and more importantly leaves you open to bad situations.

  • Accomodation

    It is best to book a room for a night or two before your arrival. That being said, look around to find the best deal once the jet-lag has faded.

  • Cultural Norms

    Be sure to know if some cultures are more conservative in their dress, or if businesses are closed on certain days of the week or for upcoming holidays.

  • Key Phrases

    You might not be familiar with the written language of these destinations, so it’s important to practice proper pronunciation and write down a few key phrases before you arrive. (Check out iTranslate Voice)

  • Drinking

    This one may be up for debate, but from what I've seen, it seems that it's recommended to not drink much (if at all).

  • Have a Plan B

    Plan for potential failure. Perhaps a credit card to get into a safe hotel, or a contact to see if they can lend you money. I'll always be open to help.

  • Be Vague

    When discussing any sort of personal information(where you're staying, where you're travelling, etc.) watch what you say. If someone asks if you have a hostel reservation, always say yes. Never trust anyone 100%.

  • Be Prepared for Sickness

    Look into perhaps getting some forms of immunization just in case. I'm not sure how significant this is, but you can look into it and plan for it.

  • Stay Healthy

    Eat well, sleep well, and stay hydrated. Don't wear yourself out. You don't want to get sick on your trip!

  • Wear a Smile

    It can be difficult to maintain a smile when people are surrounding you trying to sell you things. People react more kindly to smiles. You have a beautiful smile, so I'm sure you'll be okay. Use it!

  • Don't Be Too Cheap

    Stay at places with high ratings and don't always go for the lowest price. You can use Hostelbookers to search for locations. Also, try to enter new locations during the day, rather than at night.

  • Watch Your Belongings

    When riding on busses, wear bags with thick straps and strap them across your body. Also, try to wear appropriate clothing. I think you'll be fine for that though.
