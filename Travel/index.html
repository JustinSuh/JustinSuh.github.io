My List of Things to Know

I'm going to create a list of things that hopefully you've already heard. I've gone through a ton of websites and I hope to be able to impart enough knowledge onto you.

  • Be Excited

    As much as I would love to embark on this trip with you, I can't. From the numberous blogs, websites, and posts I've read, travelling alone is a really great opportunity. I'm sure when all is said and done, you'll have had a great time.

  • Follow Your Gut/Intuition

    Follow your gut instinct, if it feels like a bad situation, don't wait around. Move to a better situation. Trust yourself, and don't fall into panic in unfamiliar situations. Remember, just close your eyes, take a deep breath for 3 seconds in, hold it for 3 secons, and exhale over 3 seconds.

  • Prevent Creepers

    This advice I found to be rather common, and it actually makes a lot of sense. Wear a wedding ring, to keep some of the creepers at bay. It'll definitely help.

  • Don't bring valuables

    This one is pretty obvious, but leave any flashy jewelry at home, along with a huge purse if you have one. Try to keep electronics and your wallet out of sight.

  • Try to Move as a Group

    It's often more comforting to find co-ed groups to travel with for short stints. By going to a hostel and chatting it up with people, you're sure to find some adventures to join up with. (Don't be afraid to ask, or learn how to lead the conversation into an invitation).

  • Your Weapons

    Leave the mace/weapons/etc at home. It won't cross borders. If you can buy some across borders, feel free to do so. Whatever helps you feel safe. I strongly recommend keeping a whistle handy. This will most likely deter a mjority of people who mean harm.

  • Be Confident

    Don't be the victim. This has a lot to do with your presence, posture and demeanor. If you're alone and timid/skittish/etc, then you're "prey". Instead, imagine yourself as the predator and walk with your chin up and alert. This one may be tough for introverts like us, especially since you'll be somewhere new.Show off your confidence though amd make it known that you're strong, because you are.

  • Martial Arts

    That being said, if you have some spare time, some basic women-specific hand-to-hand defense training can help. If anything, it might give you some more confidence out there and help keep that chin up. This isn't totally necessary. It's more of a "nice to have".

  • Plan Your Routes

    Chose your routes and plans smartly. Going out for a drink? Go out early and get back early, or invite some hostel mates. Going out late and then taking the "short cut" through an alley on the way back to the hostel is just bad for business, and more importantly leaves you open to bad situations.
